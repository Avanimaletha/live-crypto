Cryptocurrency is a type of digital or virtual currency that relies on cryptography for security and operates on decentralized networks based on blockchain technology. Unlike traditional currencies, it is not controlled by a central authority like a government or bank, which allows for peer-to-peer transactions without intermediaries. Cryptocurrency offers the potential for faster, more efficient global transactions and enhanced financial inclusion, especially for those without access to traditional banking. Its importance lies in its potential to revolutionize industries by providing a secure, transparent, and tamper-resistant record-keeping system. However, its volatility and regulatory challenges continue to shape its role in the global economy
